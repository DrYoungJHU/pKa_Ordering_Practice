Remember, the lower the pKa, the stronger the acid, and the more acidic the proton.

Recall the general order of functional group pKa values:

↓ Strong acids (HCl, H₂SO₄) pKa < 0
↓ Carboxylic acids (RCOOH) pKa ≈ 3-5
↓ Phenols (ArOH) pKa ≈ 10
↓ Water / Alcohols (ROH) pKa ≈ 15-16
↓ Carbonyls (α-H) pKa ≈ 19-20
↓ Terminal Alkynes pKa ≈ 25
↓ Ammonia / Amines pKa ≈ 35-40
↓ Aromatics / Alkenes pKa ≈ 43-44
↓ Alkanes pKa ≈ 50+
